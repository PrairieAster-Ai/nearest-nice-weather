🚨 Risk Assessment & Mitigation

Critical risks identified and addressed in development planning

CRITICAL Platform Dependency Risk
External platform API changes or policy modifications could disrupt core functionality. Historical precedent includes major platform shutdowns affecting dependent applications.
✅ "Drought tolerant" - own our data sources
✅ Direct partnerships with actual humans who answer phones
✅ Community contributors > social media scraping
HIGH Data Access Restrictions
Platform rate limiting and terms of service changes may restrict data collection capabilities. Legal compliance requirements continue to evolve.
✅ Use web scraping for research only (6 month max)
✅ Build real partnerships before we get sued
✅ Users > bots for data collection
MEDIUM Technical Complexity
Multi-source architecture may introduce unnecessary complexity, impacting development velocity and maintenance requirements.
✅ Plugins not frameworks - keep it stupid simple
✅ Tests that actually test useful things
✅ Monitoring that tells us WHY it broke
MEDIUM Feature Scope Expansion
Tendency to add non-essential features may dilute core value proposition and increase cognitive load for users.
✅ 3-second rule: if you can't scan it, kill it
✅ Show basics first, fancy stuff later
✅ Let users turn off the noise

📋 Strategic Insights & Requirements

User research findings and technical architecture decisions

🎯 MVP Scope Definition

  • Core Value: "Where can I get tacos when it's nice out?" (Everything else is feature creep)
  • User Focus: Weekend Warriors on a budget, not enterprise travel managers
  • Technical Approach: Build boring stuff that works before building cool stuff that breaks
  • Risk Mitigation: Don't build on platforms owned by megalomaniacs

🌐 Data Strategy Reality Check

MVP: Government APIs (free) + humans who know stuff

Growth: More sources = less vendor lock-in

Post-MVP: Plugins, not platforms

Resilience: Platform survives Twitter moments
